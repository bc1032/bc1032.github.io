Talking to My Kids About Science: Bridging Generations Through Discovery

As a scientist and a parent, I've embarked on a delightful journey — talking to my kids about science. This initiative is more than just a pastime; it's a bridge connecting the world of complex scientific ideas to the curious minds of the younger generation. I believe that nurturing a person's natural curiosity about the world around them is not just a way to educate; but to inspire.

The reason I want to do this is simple yet profound. In an age where science and technology are integral to our everyday lives, understanding basic scientific principles is more important than ever. However, this understanding doesn't need to be laden with jargon or overly complex theories. By breaking down these concepts into fun, engaging, and digestible pieces, I aim to ignite a spark of scientific interest in my children — an interest that could grow into a passion or even a career.

But why is it important? Because today's children are tomorrow's leaders, innovators, and problem-solvers. By introducing them to science in a way that's accessible and enjoyable, I hope to equip them with not just knowledge, but a way of thinking. Critical thinking, problem-solving, and a never-ending quest for understanding are just as crucial in everyday life as they are in scientific endeavours. In the age of mistruth, bizarrely enabled by the thinkers of yesterday; the thinkers of tomorrow can find it difficult to navigate their way through the complexities of who and what to believe.

"Talking to My Kids About Science" is more than just an educational project; it's about sharing the wonder, the logic, and the beauty of science. It's about showing them that science is not just about experiments and equations; it's about exploring the universe, understanding how things work, and asking 'why' and 'how' at every step. Join us on this journey, and let's explore the fascinating world of Science together!

Who the hell are you?

At the heart of this project is the idea that you should never trust anything that you're told at face value; I believe that everyone should question things. Everyone should inform themselves. Therefore, if you're anything like me, you are already questioning who I am and how I can speak with any authority on such matters! It's only right and fair then that I should introduce myself, and tell you who the hell I am.

It's a great question! I'm a passionate explorer of the universe, at least the tiny part of it that's covered by theoretical physics and soft matter. As a PhD student in Theoretical Physics, researching the world of "Soft Matter" and Topology, at the University of Bristol, I've spent years untangling the complexities of the physical world. I have spent countless hours trying to boil down seemingly difficult ideas and concepts, and understand them at their most fundamental level.

My journey in science began with an MSci (Integrated Masters) in Physics, followed by diving a little deeper into Mathematics, because why stop at one degree when the mysteries of the universe are calling? This path led me to specialise in the fascinating world of Soft Matter, where I get to explore the wild and wonderful behaviours of materials that are neither entirely solid nor completely liquid. From goo to poo

But what does this all mean? In simple terms, I study materials that have the power to change the way we interact with technology and understand the natural world. From the screens of our smartphones to the mysteries of the diverse patterns and aesthetic beauty inherent in nature, my work explores the intricate dance of molecules, and the strange properties they exhibit under various conditions.

I'm not just about equations and lab coats, though. As a dedicated parent and an enthusiast of science communication, I believe in breaking down complex concepts into fun and digestible chunks. Why? Because science is for everyone! In the words of one of my great heroes Carl Sagan: "We are made of star-stuff. [Science is a] way for the Universe to know itself!" It's a thrilling, ever-evolving story, and I'm here to share its most exciting chapters with you.

So, there you have it — a scientist, educator, and storyteller, all rolled into one. I'm here to guide you through the wonders of science, one fascinating topic at a time.
